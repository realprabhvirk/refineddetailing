------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS — CAR DETAILING
------------------------------------------------------------
- Shoot in the early morning (golden hour) or on overcast days.
  Harsh midday sun creates glare and kills detail in paint shots.
- Use a reflective surface test: good lighting = you can see
  definition in the reflections. Bad lighting = flat, dull paint.
- Always shoot AFTER detail work, not during (unless intentional).
- A dark/black vehicle shows polish and reflection best.
- Get low — bonnet-level shots feel dramatic and premium.
- Avoid shooting near cluttered backgrounds (neighbour's bins, etc.).
  A driveway with a plain fence or lawn behind works well.
- For interior shots: shoot from the passenger side looking into
  the driver area, or from the rear seat looking forward.
- Keep your phone/camera lens clean. Smudges kill sharpness.
- For social media ready shots: 1:1 ratio works for Instagram,
  16:9 for Facebook and website banners.

------------------------------------------------------------
COMPRESSION & OPTIMISATION TOOLS
------------------------------------------------------------
squoosh.app         — Google's free browser-based image compressor.
                      Best for JPG and WebP conversion. Use "MozJPEG"
                      at 80–85 quality for great balance.

tinyjpg.com         — Drag and drop JPG/PNG compression. Fast and easy.

imageresizer.com    — Quick resize before compressing.

TIP: Convert JPGs to WebP format where possible for 25–35% smaller
file sizes with no visible quality loss. The site is coded to support
standard JPG/PNG — if you switch to WebP, just update the src
attributes in the HTML accordingly.

REPLACE PEXELS IMAGES ASAP
------------------------------------------------------------
The site currently loads images from Pexels as temporary placeholders.
These are royalty-free but generic — they're not your actual work.

Your own before/after shots will:

Even 6–8 real job photos makes a huge difference.
